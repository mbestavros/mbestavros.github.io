Work

Current: Red Hat

Current: Red Hat

I've been at Red Hat since October 2018, when I joined as a part-time intern while finishing up my graduate studies at Boston University. I moved to full-time in June 2019.

I'm part of the Office of the CTO's Emerging Technologies group, working on the Security team. Our job is to identify nascent open-source security projects with long-term strategic value to Red Hat and bootstrap them into successful upstream communities.

In recent times, we've had a particularly strong focus on software supply chain security. Our team helped co-found the very trendy Sigstore project (which you can read about on my blog here!), and we've been primary contributors to the Keylime project since its inception. I've done work with both these efforts during my recent time at Red Hat. In the past, I've also contributed to the Enarx project.

Summer 2018: MITRE

I interned at MITRE during the summer of 2018, working in their Agile Software Engineering division.

I worked on the Provider Screening Module, an open-source project to screen healthcare providers for eligibility in different states. I also contributed to FluxNotes, another open-source project to create structured data out of clinical notes.

I was also fortunate enough to participate in the MITRE eCTF for summer interns.

Summer 2017: VMware

I interned at VMware for the summer of 2017 in their Cambridge (now Boston) office.

While there, I worked on prototyping the company's Workspace ONE Android app, a BYOD mobile device management solution for businesses to deploy workplace apps on employee personal phones.
