Personal

Here's a look behind the curtain: a few of my hobbies and interests. If you're looking to strike up a conversation with me, these are great places to start.

Trombone

{% responsiveImage, "img/bu-pep-band-trombones.jpg", "" %}

Trombone

The BU Pep Band brass section, playing our hearts out at a Men's Ice Hockey game. I'm fourth from right in the back row (first one with a golden trombone). Photo courtesy of BU Bands.

I've been playing trombone since the fourth grade, and I continued playing it all the way through college. The BU Pep Band was an absolute blast to be a part of. I haven't quite found an outlet for the skill post-graduation, but I'm always on the lookout.

Gaming

{% responsiveImage, "img/personal/boston-major-silhouette.jpg", "" %}

I like playing video games. I play on PC, and my favorites are:
  • Skyrim -- not only do I play (a lot), I also write mods for it in my spare time.
  • Civilization (specifically V; though I own VI, I haven't gotten into it yet)
  • Counter-Strike: Global Offensive (silver for life!)
  • The Sims (on occasion, guilty pleasure)
  • Classic Need for Speed (Underground 2, Most Wanted, Carbon, ProStreet)
  • Forza (Motorsport and Horizon, though I prefer the latter)
Generally, if it's moddable, I'll probably enjoy it.

I also follow Counter-Strike esports quite closely. I owe that to the incredible 2018 Eleague Boston Major, which I was lucky enough to attend live at Agganis Arena. The above picture is from just before the finals.

Consumer tech and gadgets

{% responsiveImage, "img/personal/project-fi-care-package.jpg", "" %}

I consider myself a power user, and I'm awfully particular about the tech I use every day.

I love reading reviews and thoughts on new gadgets, and I'm always on the lookout for new ways to tinker with my devices. I've always had a passing interest in writing about these things, and my blog will likely become a manifestation of that desire.

The above picture is one of the two Project Fi (now Google Fi) care packages I received for being an early adopter. (It's a great service, especially if you travel internationally a lot.)

Star (Trek/Wars)

{% responsiveImage, "img/personal/millennium-falcon.jpg", "" %}

Above: The Millennium Falcon in Disneyland Anaheim's "Star Wars: Galaxy's Edge" park.

Though I do like Star Wars (a lot), I actually consider myself more of a Trekkie. (I just couldn't find a good Star Trek-related picture of mine.)

TNG is probably my favorite, followed by DS9 and Voyager in a tie. For movies, I really liked First Contact, Undiscovered Country, and Voyage Home. I like the New-Trek movies for what they are. I still have not seen Discovery or Picard, though I really want to.

Trains (specifically, the MBTA)

{% responsiveImage, "img/personal/lansdowne-commuter-rail-skyline.jpg", "" %}

Man, I love trains. Loved them as a kid, still love them now.

My current normal commute involves the MBTA Commuter Rail every day, and I enjoy the extra time I get back from it. My ultimate goal is to live somewhere on the MBTA's subway system and not have to own a car at all.

Because I take the MBTA so often, I find it fascinating to dig into the history of Boston's subway and read about the region's transport infrastructure. I'm game to talk your ear off about it if you let me.
