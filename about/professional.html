Current: Red Hat

Current: Red Hat

I've been at Red Hat since October 2018, when I joined as a part-time intern while finishing up my graduate studies at Boston University. I moved to full-time in June 2019.

I'm currently working on Enarx, a project aiming to provide an abstraction layer across different implementations of trusted execution environments (TEEs) from major hardware vendors, like Intel SGX and AMD SEV. This will allow developers to write applications for third-party cloud vendors without needing to trust the host's stack.

The project's goal is to make developing for TEEs not just easier, but more portable. You shouldn't need to rewrite (or even recompile) your app for a different TEE.

The project is fully open-source and open-process: all our meetings and issue tracking are public, and you can find out more at https://enarx.io.
